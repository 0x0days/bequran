Quranic Numerics · Mountains as Pegs

The Quran called mountains "pegs" — a word implying deep underground roots — 1,400 years before geologists discovered that mountains extend hundreds of kilometres below ground.

78:6–7 — Surah An-Nabaʾ · The Announcement
أَلَمْ نَجْعَلِ الْأَرْضَ مِهَادًا ۝ وَالْجِبَالَ أَوْتَادًا
"Have We not made the Earth a resting place, and the mountains as pegs?"

A peg is an anchor: a small visible tip above ground and a much longer shaft driven deep below. The Quran chose this exact word for mountains. Geologists in 2010 confirmed that the Indian plate was forced 250 km down into the mantle beneath the Himalayas — roots more than 28 times deeper than Everest is tall. The word أوتادًا captures this structure precisely.

The precision of the word: Arabic has many words for mountain. The Quran specifically chose أوتاد — pegs. This word is only accurate if mountains have a large hidden structure below the surface, which was completely unknown in the 7th century. Seismology and the theory of isostasy only emerged in the 20th century.
"And the mountains as pegs" — the single most accurate description of mountain structure in any 7th-century text.
A tent peg is mostly underground. A mountain's root can be 28 times deeper than its visible height. No ancient geographer, cartographer, or natural philosopher described mountains this way — because they had no way to know.
