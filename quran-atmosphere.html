Chest Tightens as You Rise

In 624 CE, nobody had climbed above 8,000 metres. Nobody knew that altitude crushes the chest. The Quran used this as a universally-understood comparison centuries before atmospheric science existed.

Quran 6:125
وَمَنْ يُرِدْ أَنْ يُضِلَّهُ يَجْعَلْ صَدْرَهُ ضَيِّقًا حَرَجًا كَأَنَّمَا يَصَّعَّدُ فِي السَّمَاءِ
"And those whom He wants to leave astray, He makes their chests tight and constricted, as if they are ascending to the sky."
Why this is remarkable
The Quran uses altitude as an analogy for chest tightness implying that ascending causes constriction of breathing. This is the exact physiological reality: as altitude increases, atmospheric pressure drops, oxygen thins, and the chest physically tightens. The Death Zone begins at 8,000 metres where pressure falls below 356 millibars and humans cannot survive without supplemental oxygen.
Sea level: 1,013 mbar normal breathing, chest free
4,000 m: ~617 mbar shortness of breath begins
8,000 m (Death Zone): <356 mbar human life unsustainable without oxygen

The Sky That Crushes

Metres Death Zone
No one in 624 CE had ascended to the Death Zone. No instruments existed to measure atmospheric pressure. No one had scientifically established that altitude causes chest constriction. Yet the Quran used ascending to the sky as a self-evident metaphor for exactly that sensation describing it as progressive (يَصَّعَّدُ) and physically precise.

The word for "constricted" (ضَيِّقًا حَرَجًا) combines two degrees of tightening narrow then severely compressed. This is a medically accurate description of the stages of altitude hypoxia: first shortness of breath, then complete respiratory failure.
Wikipedia · Effects of High Altitude on Humans · 2019
"The death zone, in mountaineering, refers to altitudes above a certain point where the amount of oxygen is insufficient to sustain human life for an extended time span. This point is generally tagged as 8,000 m (26,000 ft, less than 356 millibars of atmospheric pressure)."
