Spider Web

The Quran uses the female verb form for who builds the spider's web and calls it the most fragile of houses. Science confirmed both facts 1,400 years later.

29:41 · Surah Al-Ankabut
مَثَلُ الَّذِينَ اتَّخَذُوا مِنْ دُونِ اللَّهِ أَوْلِيَاءَ كَمَثَلِ الْعَنْكَبُوتِ اتَّخَذَتْ بَيْتًا ۖ وَإِنَّ أَوْهَنَ الْبُيُوتِ لَبَيْتُ الْعَنْكَبُوتِ ۖ لَوْ كَانُوا يَعْلَمُونَ
"The likeness of those who take protectors other than Allah is that of the spider. She builds a house. But the most fragile of houses is the spider's house. If they only knew."
The Discovery
The verb اتَّخَذَتْ (ittakhathat) is the female form the male would be اتَّخَذَ (ittakhadha). Arachnology confirms: adult female spiders build typical webs; adult males do not. Males reserve their silk for mating alone. Furthermore, the web is damaged by wind, rain, dust, and every captured prey requiring up to 20 meters of silk to rebuild each night.

The Quran used the female verb and called the web the most fragile house.

The verse could have used the male verb form اتَّخَذَ it would still be understood as "the spider built." Instead it used اتَّخَذَتْ, the female form, precisely matching the biological fact that only female spiders build typical webs. Combined with the description of the web as "the most fragile of houses" a structure destroyed by wind, rain, dust, and every single catch, requiring 20 meters of fresh silk each night this is not a guess. It is knowledge.
