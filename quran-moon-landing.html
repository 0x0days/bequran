Why the Hijri calendar? The Quran's community has always measured time by the lunar Hijri calendar — not the Gregorian. 1389 AH is not a "conversion trick." It is the actual year of the moon landing in the calendar the Quran was revealed into. And 1,389 is the exact verse count, requiring zero rounding.
The Quran's only moon-split verse sits at position 4,847. Exactly 1,389 verses follow it. Apollo 11 landed on the moon in 1389 AH. The surah is named Al-Qamar — "The Moon."
The structure was fixed in the 7th century. The verse count is a fact (6,236 − 4,847 = 1,389). The Hijri date is historical (July 20, 1969 = 1389 AH). No selection, no rounding, no adjustment was made. The numbers meet exactly.
Probability: A random verse in the Quran being followed by a count that matches a specific historical Hijri year is approximately 1 in 6,236. The thematic coherence — the moon verse producing the moon-landing year — makes the match structurally coherent in a way that random matches are not.
