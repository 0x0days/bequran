Exercise 2 of 3 — The Semantic Method
Both words start at 24 singular forms. One verse each requires a single adjustment.
Quran 39:29 · Man — three occurrences, two roles
ضَرَبَ ٱللَّهُ مَثَلًا رَّجُلًا فِيهِ شُرَكَآءُ مُتَشَـٰكِسُونَ وَرَجُلًا سَلَمًا لِّرَجُلٍ
"A man owned by quarreling partners" and "a man belonging entirely to one master" — two parallel illustrations of the same conceptual role (the slave), plus one for the master. Three appearances, two roles.
3 occurrences → 2 semantic roles  ·  subtract 1
Quran 66:10 · Woman — two occurrences, one role
ضَرَبَ ٱللَّهُ مَثَلًا لِّلَّذِينَ كَفَرُوا۟ ٱمْرَأَتَ نُوحٍ وَٱمْرَأَتَ لُوطٍ
Wife of Noah and wife of Lot — a paired example of one conceptual role: the disbelieving wife. Two appearances forming one unified example count as one.
2 occurrences → 1 semantic role  ·  subtract 1
