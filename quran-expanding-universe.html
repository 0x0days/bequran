Lesson 53 · Cosmology

The Universe Is Expanding. The Quran Said So First.

Surah Adh-Dhariyat · 51:47
وَالسَّمَاءَ بَنَيْنَاهَا بِأَيْدٍ وَإِنَّا لَمُوسِعُونَ
"And the heaven — We built it with craftsmanship, and indeed We are expanding it."

In 1916, Albert Einstein published his Theory of General Relativity. His equations showed a universe that must be either expanding or contracting. He didn't believe that, so he added a fake term — the cosmological constant — to force the equations to produce a static universe. He later called it his greatest blunder.

In 1929, Edwin Hubble confirmed it: every galaxy is moving away from every other galaxy. The universe is expanding. Einstein retracted his cosmological constant immediately. The Bible had described the sky as "hard as a mirror of cast bronze" (Job 37:18) — a rigid, static firmament. Einstein trusted that. The Quran had said the opposite 1,400 years earlier.

The verb is present continuous — not past tense ("We built"), not future tense. It means "We are currently expanding it." Modern cosmology confirms the expansion is ongoing today, accelerating due to Dark Energy. The tense is not accidental.

Exercise 3 of 3 · Dark Energy

Something Is Pushing the Universe Apart — and We Don't Know What It Is

In 1998, three astronomers discovered that the expansion of the universe is not slowing down — it is accelerating. Something is pushing galaxies apart with increasing force the further they are from each other. They called it Dark Energy. They won the Nobel Prize in Physics in 2011. To this day, nobody knows what Dark Energy is.

What we know about Dark Energy: It makes up approximately 68% of the universe. It has a repulsive effect opposite to gravity. The further apart two galaxies are, the stronger it pushes them. It is causing the universe to expand at an accelerating rate. Beyond that — silence. The greatest open question in physics.

What is currently causing the universe to expand at an accelerating rate?

A
Gravity pulling matter outward
Gravity is attractive — it pulls matter together, not apart
B
Dark Energy — a mysterious repulsive force whose nature is still unknown
Discovered 1998. Nobel Prize 2011. Comprises ~68% of the universe. Scientists still cannot explain what it is.
C
The initial momentum of the Big Bang alone
The Big Bang's momentum would cause expansion to slow down over time, not accelerate

Three independent failures vs one correct statement:

Every pre-modern source that addressed the structure of the universe assumed it was static. The Greek model was static. The Biblical model was static. Einstein — the greatest physicist in history — assumed it was static and spent years trying to make his equations confirm it. He was wrong. The Quran used a present-continuous verb to describe ongoing expansion in a sentence that every literate 7th-century Arab would have read without understanding its cosmological implication.
Dark Energy makes it more remarkable: The Quran does not say "We expanded it" (past tense). It says "We are expanding it" — present, ongoing, active. In 1998, astronomers discovered the expansion is not just ongoing but accelerating, driven by a force — Dark Energy — that makes up 68% of the universe and whose nature remains completely unknown. The most precise word the Quran could have used was exactly the word it used.
