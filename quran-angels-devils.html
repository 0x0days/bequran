Pattern 8 · Angels & Devils

The Quran's two opposing forces.
Angels and devils — the eternal duality.

مَلَك
Malak · Angels
Divine messengers. Guardians of creation, bearers of revelation, recorders of deeds — they guide humanity toward the straight path.
شَيْطَان
Shaytan · Devils
Cosmic tempters. Dedicated to misleading, whispering doubt, adorning evil — the adversary at every turn of human life.

Multiple choice

The word مَلِك (with a kasra on the middle letter) looks almost identical to مَلَك (angel). It is excluded from the angel count of 88. Why is this NOT arbitrary?

It was included first, then subtracted to force the 88:88 balance
A single vowel mark changes the word entirely — fatḥa-fatḥa = angel (مَلَك), fatḥa-kasra = king (مَلِك). Different roots, different meanings
Kings are considered a type of angel in Islamic theology
The word for king only appears in later Medinan surahs
