The universe is 13.8 billion years old. Earth formed 4.6 billion years ago. That places Earth at exactly one third of the universe's age — 4.6 ÷ 13.8 = 0.333. The Quran states this in a different way: the whole of creation (heavens, Earth, everything between) took 6 days measured at God's Throne, while Earth itself existed for only the last 2 of those days. Two sixths equals one third — the same ratio, in a different frame of reference.

Why expressing it as a ratio matters: The universe's age is measured differently depending on the observer's speed and gravitational field (Einstein's relativity). An observer measuring the universe as 3 billion years old also measures Earth as 1 billion years old — still one third. By expressing it as a ratio rather than absolute numbers, the Quran's statement is correct for every possible observer in every frame of reference.
The Christian Bible comparison: Genesis places the creation of Earth on day one — making Earth the same age as the universe. Modern cosmology refutes this. The Quran places Earth's formation as beginning on the fourth day — after four days of prior cosmic history — which matches what cosmologists measure.
"Created the Earth in two days." Six days total. The same ratio — one third — that cosmologists measure 1,400 years later.
Cosmologists confirmed the 13.8-billion-year age of the universe in the 20th century. No human in 7th-century Arabia could have measured, calculated, or guessed that the correct ratio of Earth's age to the universe's age is precisely one third.
