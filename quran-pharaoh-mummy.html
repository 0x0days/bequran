The Discovery
Pharaoh wore heavy metal scale armor — copper, iron, and semi-precious stones. That armor would have sunk his corpse to the bottom of the Red Sea. The Bible says his body was never recovered. Yet in 1975, French scientist Dr. Maurice Bucaille found Ramesses II's mummy preserved inland — with salt crystals still in its tissue, confirming he drowned. The Quran predicted this 1,400 years earlier, word for word.

Dr. Maurice Bucaille — French Surgeon & Scientist
"I could not explain how the author of the Quran could have known about the preservation of Pharaoh's body without modern medical knowledge. After comparing the Quran and science rigorously, I declared my acceptance of Islam."
Pharaoh's metal scale armor — copper, iron, and semi-precious stones — is denser than water. His body should have sunk irretrievably to the seabed. The Bible claims the body was simply gone. Yet the Quran alone stated in 610 AD: "Today We will preserve your body as a sign."

In 1975, Dr. Bucaille examined the mummy of Ramesses II and found salt crystals embedded in the tissue — proof of drowning. The body had been found in the Valley of the Kings, hundreds of miles from the sea. An illiterate man in 7th-century Arabia could not have known about a preserved Pharaoh's mummy. No mistakes in the Quran.
