Quranic Numerics · Cosmology

A single verse in the Quran describes the singularity, the expansion, and water-based life — three 20th-century discoveries in one sentence.

Quran 21:30 — Surah Al-Anbiya · The Prophets
أَوَلَمْ يَرَ الَّذِينَ كَفَرُوا أَنَّ السَّمَاوَاتِ وَالْأَرْضَ كَانَتَا رَتْقًا فَفَتَقْنَاهُمَا ۖ وَجَعَلْنَا مِنَ الْمَاءِ كُلَّ شَيْءٍ حَيٍّ
"Do those who disbelieve not see that the heavens and the Earth were meshed together then We ripped them apart? And that We made from water every living thing?"

The Big Bang theory — confirmed by cosmic background radiation in 1964 — describes the universe beginning as an infinitely compressed singularity, then exploding outward. The Quran uses two precise Arabic words: ratq (رَتْقًا) meaning "tight and compact, meshed together," and fatq (فَتَقْنَاهُمَا) meaning "torn apart by pulling in opposite directions" — the exact mechanism of dark energy, which stretches the fabric of spacetime outward in all directions.

Exercise 2 of 3

Quran 21:30 contains three discoveries. Reveal them all.

💥
رَتْقًا
The Big Bang singularity
Before the Big Bang, all matter and energy in the universe occupied a single, infinitely compressed point — a singularity. Ratq means "sealed, tight, and compact, meshed as one." The Quran says the heavens and Earth were in this state before creation.
🌌
فَتَقْنَاهُمَا
Dark Energy — pulling spacetime apart
Fatq means to tear cloth by pulling the fabric in opposite directions — not an explosion, but a stretching. This is precisely how dark energy works: it does not push matter outward, it stretches the fabric of spacetime itself in all directions simultaneously.
💧
مِنَ الْمَاءِ كُلَّ شَيْءٍ حَيٍّ
All living things are water-based
"We made from water every living thing." This is the same verse — Quran 21:30. The discovery that all known life requires liquid water, that cells are mostly water, and that water is the universal solvent of biology was confirmed in the 19th and 20th centuries.
Three discoveries. One verse.
أَنَّ السَّمَاوَاتِ وَالْأَرْضَ كَانَتَا رَتْقًا فَفَتَقْنَاهُمَا
The singularity, the expansion, and water-based life — all three confirmed by modern science, all three in Quran 21:30.

Why "fatq" is not just "separation": Arabic has many words for separating or moving apart. The word fatq specifically describes the action of tearing a garment by pulling its fabric in opposite directions — stretching and ripping. This is not an explosion pushing things outward; it is a stretching of the fabric itself. This is precisely the mechanism of dark energy, which stretches spacetime rather than pushing matter.
The water claim in context: In 7th-century Arabia, most scholars held that fire and earth were the primary elements of life. The Quran's claim that water is the basis of all life contradicted the common understanding — and was confirmed by modern biology. Every living cell requires water. The discovery of water on other planets is now a primary indicator in the search for extraterrestrial life.
"The heavens and the Earth were meshed together, then We ripped them apart." The singularity and the expansion — in one sentence, from the 7th century.
Georges Lemaître proposed the Big Bang in 1927. Arno Penzias and Robert Wilson confirmed it with cosmic microwave background radiation in 1964. The Quran described the same event 1,300 years earlier using vocabulary that matches the physics with remarkable precision.
