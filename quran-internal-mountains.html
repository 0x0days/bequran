For most of history, people assumed Quranic mountains meant surface mountains the kind you can see. But the Arabic word chosen is فِي (fee), meaning inside. In 1994, seismic wave scanning revealed a massive mountain range buried 410 miles inside Earth's mantle up to 7 times taller than Everest.

Arabic has two prepositions for location. Which one does the Quran choose for Earth's mountains?

The Arabic word فِي (fee) in verse 21:31 means…

A
On top of the surface
The word عَلَى (aala) would be used for this meaning
B
Inside / within the Earth
فِي is the standard Arabic preposition for "inside" or "within"
C
Beneath the ocean floor
A more specific phrase would be required for this location

Word choice matters. The Quran did not say mountains on the Earth (عَلَى) it said mountains فِي (inside). That single preposition distinguishes a folk observation from a precise geological claim made 14 centuries before it could be verified.
Function confirmed. The Quran's phrase "lest it sways" directly maps to the modern discovery that the 410-mile boundary zone anchors the viscous upper mantle, preventing large-scale convective instability a stabilizing role invisible from the surface.
"And We placed inside the Earth firm mountains, lest it sway with them." Quran 21:31
One word. One preposition. One claim that every geologist in the 7th century would have called impossible and that seismology in 1994 confirmed in full.
