Quranic Numerics · Carbon Creation

Carbon is the building block of all life — atomic number 6, mass number 12. The Quran's creation vocabulary contains exactly 6 distinct materials. The creation word for clay appears exactly 12 times.

Every atom has an atomic number — the count of protons in its nucleus. Carbon's is 6, which is why it's element 6 on the periodic table. Carbon also has a mass number — the total count of protons plus neutrons. The most stable, abundant form of carbon is C-12: 6 protons and 6 neutrons, mass number 12. These two numbers — 6 and 12 — define carbon. They were first precisely established in the 19th and 20th centuries. The Quran was recorded in the 7th century.

Why carbon? Carbon is the only atom that can form the long, stable chains needed for DNA, proteins, and sugars. Every amino acid, every nucleotide, every sugar molecule has a carbon backbone. Carbon-12 is the most abundant isotope — it is literally the "clay" of life at the atomic level.
Six creation materials — the Quran's vocabulary for the substance of human existence — match the six protons that define the atom all life is built from. The clay word counts twelve, matching the mass of carbon's most stable isotope.
