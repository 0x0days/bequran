Prewired at Birth
The very first word ever revealed in the Quran is اقْرَأْ — "Read." The passage describes creation from a leech (before birth), then immediately: taught to read by the pen. The sequence implies the capacity for reading was given before birth.

For 1,400 years this seemed like a spiritual command. Then neuroscientists scanned newborn brains and found something extraordinary: the Visual Word Form Area (VWFA) — the brain's reading center — is already connected to the language network at birth, before any exposure to words. The human brain is born prewired to read.

For 1,400 years, scholars read "اقْرَأْ — Read" as a divine command. Nobody thought it said anything about biology. The conventional belief was that reading is a skill acquired years after birth — a product of education, not nature.

In 2020, Ohio State University neuroscientists published MRI scans of newborn brains. They found the Visual Word Form Area — the brain region responsible for reading — is already functionally connected to the language network at birth. Before the infant has seen a single word. Before any language exposure. The reading circuit is installed before birth.

Quran 96 opens: "Created man from a leech" (embryonic stage) → "taught by the pen" (reading, before birth). The verse that commanded an illiterate man to "Read" described the neurological prewiring of the human brain 1,400 years before it was discovered.
