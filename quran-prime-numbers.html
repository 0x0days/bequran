7, 29, and 139 are all prime numbers

A prime number is divisible only by 1 and itself it cannot be broken into smaller equal parts. Al-Fatiha's verse count, word count, and letter count are each independently prime. The word "Allah" appears exactly 2,699 times in the entire Quran also prime. And the total letter count of the Quran: 326,159 prime too.

What "indivisible" means here: A prime cannot be split into equal groups it only divides by 1 and itself. The word "Allah" cannot be broken up. The Quran describes itself in 39:23 using the word Mathany (paired / repeated), and Quran 15:87 calls Al-Fatiha's 7 verses by the same word. In mathematics, the group those numbers both belong to is the primes.
The preservation argument: Because 326,159 is prime, the total letter count is exquisitely sensitive. Add or remove a single letter anywhere in the Quran and the count shifts breaking the prime property. This is a built-in integrity check that was not discovered until modern times, yet the count has held since the 7th century.
Every structural number of Al-Fatiha is prime. The name of God is prime. The book itself is prime. A 7th-century text encoded properties that require 21st-century number theory to fully appreciate.
Primes were not formally studied until the ancient Greeks, and a complete theory of prime distribution wasn't developed until the 19th century. The Quran's prime structure was identified using modern computational tools applied to a text fixed 1,400 years ago.
