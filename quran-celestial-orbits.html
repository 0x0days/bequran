Lesson · Celestial Orbits

Lesson 51 · Astronomy

All Are Swimming in an Orbit

Surah Al-Anbiya · 21:33
وَهُوَ ٱلَّذِى خَلَقَ ٱلَّيْلَ وَٱلنَّهَارَ وَٱلشَّمْسَ وَٱلْقَمَرَ ۖ كُلٌّۭ فِى فَلَكٍۢ يَسْبَحُونَ
"It is He who created the night and the day, and the sun and the moon — all are swimming in an orbit."

In 1543, Copernicus waited until his deathbed to publish that Earth orbits the Sun. In 1633, Galileo was sentenced to house arrest for confirming it. For over a thousand years before either of them, humanity assumed Earth was fixed and everything revolved around it. The Quran stated otherwise — openly, in the 7th century.

This lesson covers 4 discoveries hidden in two verses: the Arabic word for orbit, the motion of Earth and Moon, an Arabic grammar proof that Earth moves, and the Sun's own orbit around the Milky Way — all described 1,400 years before modern astronomy confirmed them.

Exercise 1 of 4 · The Word

The Arabic Word for Orbit

The Quran uses the word فَلَكٍۢ (falak). Watch what happens when you see how this word sits in the phrase كُلٌّۭ فِى فَلَكٍۢ — "all in an orbit." The letter ى from فِى sits at the center. The letters of فَلَكٍۢ orbit around it — the very concept of orbital motion encoded in the language itself.

Exercise 3 of 4 · The Grammar Proof

The Plural That Proves Earth Moves

Arabic grammar has three distinct number forms — singular, dual, and plural. The Quran is always precise. In Quran 39:5, Allah says:

Surah Az-Zumar · 39:5
وَسَخَّرَ الشَّمْسَ وَالْقَمَرَ ۖ كُلٌّ يَجْرِي لِأَجَلٍ مُّسَمًّى
"He enslaved the sun and the moon — ALL move to a prerecorded destiny."

The verse mentions sun and moon — two bodies. But look at the grammar it uses:

كِلَاهُمَا يَجْرِيَانِ
DUAL — "Both move"
Would refer to exactly 2 things: sun + moon
✗ NOT what 39:5 says
كُلٌّ يَجْرِي
PLURAL — "All move" (3 or more)
Sun + Moon + at least one more = Earth
✓ WHAT THE QURAN SAYS
The proof: If only the sun and moon moved, the grammatically correct Arabic would be dual form. By using the plural كُلٌّ (kullun), the Quran is saying three or more bodies move — explicitly including Earth, 1,000 years before Copernicus.

In Arabic, كُلٌّ يَجْرِي (kullun yajree) grammatically refers to:

A
Exactly two things (sun and moon)
That would require the dual form: كلاهما يجريان
B
Only one thing (the moon)
C
Three or more things — Earth is included
Arabic plural means 3+. Since only sun and moon are named, the third must be Earth

Exercise 4 of 4 · The Galaxy

Even the Sun Is Orbiting Something

The Quran said كُلٌّ — ALL. Modern astronomy pushed this further: the Sun itself does not stand still. It travels through the Milky Way galaxy at 828,000 km/h, orbiting the galactic center — a supermassive black hole 26,000 light-years away.

This was discovered by astronomers Bertil Lindblad and Jan Oort in the 1920s — 1,300 years after the Quran declared that all celestial bodies move. كُلٌّ يَجْرِي — all move: the Moon around Earth, Earth around the Sun, the Sun around the galaxy. The Quran was not speaking loosely.

The Quran did not say "the sun and moon orbit." It said ALL move — using the plural form that demands three or more, in a society that believed Earth was fixed.
Four facts in two verses: The word فَلَكٍۢ precisely means orbit. The verb يَسْبَحُونَ (they swim) describes continuous orbital motion. The plural كُلٌّ mathematically requires Earth as a third moving body. And the Sun itself orbits the Milky Way — discovered 1,300 years after these words were spoken.
The question that remains: How does a 7th-century merchant in Arabia, with no telescopes, no mathematics of planetary motion, and no access to astronomy, describe orbital mechanics with grammatical precision — using the exact correct Arabic plural to include Earth among the moving bodies?
