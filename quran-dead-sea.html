Quranic Numerics · Dead Sea

The Quran described a battle at "the lowest land" — 1,400 years before science could measure which land on Earth was actually the lowest.

30:2–3 — Surah Ar-Rūm · The Romans
غُلِبَتِ الرُّومُ ۝ فِي أَدْنَى الْأَرْضِ وَهُمْ مِنْ بَعْدِ غَلَبِهِمْ سَيَغْلِبُونَ
"The Romans were defeated in the lowest land, and they, after this defeat, will be victorious."

The word أدنى (adnā) has two meanings in Arabic: "nearest" and "lowest." For centuries, scholars read it as "nearest land" — the closest Roman territory. Modern geography reveals something extraordinary: the Dead Sea, where this battle took place, is also Earth's lowest land point — 430 meters below sea level. Both meanings are true. Neither could have been verified in the 7th century.
