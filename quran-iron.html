Iron was used for centuries before the Quran. Yet the Quran made a precise claim: iron was sent down from the heavens (وَأَنزَلْنَا). At the time, this seemed wrong all iron clearly came from the ground. Today astrophysics confirms: iron cannot form on Earth through any chemical reaction. It is forged only in the cores of massive dying stars. When those stars explode as supernovae, they hurl iron across the universe. Meteorites carry it to Earth literally from the sky. The Quran's verb "sent down" is not poetic. It is astrophysically exact. And inside that same verse, three numbers each align with a real scientific measurement of iron.

Not a coincidence three independent measurements: The word's gematria, the chapter's number, and a verse count all resolve to iron-specific facts.
