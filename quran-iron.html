Quranic Numerics · Iron

The Quran encoded three separate measurements about iron into a single chapter — 1,400 years before science could measure any of them.

Iron was known and used long before the Quran, yet the Quran called it "sent down" — a claim that seemed absurd when everything iron touched was clearly made on Earth. Today, astrophysics confirms: iron cannot be produced on Earth. It is forged inside stars and scattered by supernovas. Three numbers hidden in the Quran's description of iron each match a real scientific measurement.

Not a coincidence — three independent measurements: The word's gematria, the chapter's number, and a verse count all resolve to iron-specific facts. The gematria of حديد (8+4+10+4=26) equals iron's atomic number. Chapter 57 matches both isotope ⁵⁷Fe and the gematria of الحديد (1+30+8+4+10+4=57). The verse count to 57:25 from the Quran's start matches the depth of Earth's solid iron core to the kilometer.
Iron cannot form on Earth — only inside dying stars. The Quran said it was "sent down" fourteen centuries before astrophysics confirmed this, and embedded three verifiable numbers about it into the same chapter.
Iron has the strongest bound nucleus among all elements — it is the endpoint of stellar nuclear fusion. Stars that try to fuse iron beyond it collapse. "Great might" is not poetic; it is the most precise nuclear description possible.
